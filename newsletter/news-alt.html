Executive coach guides traders out of pits
Jody Michael knows their pain; she once lost everything in the Chicago Board of Trade pits

Melissa Harris
CHICAGO CONFIDENTIAL
January 27, 2010

By the time traders turn to executive coach Jody Michael for help, they are "angry, frustrated, confused, scared, vulnerable, anxious, not sleeping and often medicated," she says. And once in a while, she will reveal to a client that she understands his pain. In 1987, at age 29, Michael and her business partner lost everything they had by placing the wrong bets in the pits at the Chicago Board of Trade.

After the crash, her partner fled to an ashram, where she spent a decade practicing yoga and meditation in near seclusion. After the initial shock wore off, Michael quickly rewrote her resume and, within two days, was hired as a floor manager at a well-respected firm. Now, her life has rebounded to the point that she can no longer say with certainty which pit — possibly it was live cattle — did her in.

"If they're coming into my office, they're at their last straw," Michael said. "They've spent their career focused externally, on trading and the marketplace, and what they do not do, to their own detriment, is focus on themselves."
