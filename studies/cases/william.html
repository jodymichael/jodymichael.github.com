Case Studies :: Life Coaching

William: Life Turbulence

Situation:
William approached Jody Michael & Associates with a decision he'd been struggling with for many months. He was unhappy in his marriage. However, he did not want to turn the lives of his three children upside-down.

Resolution:
To start, Jody Michael and William examined his inner dialogue for clues that might favor one course of action or another. Should William initiate divorce? Should he try to get custody of his children? Could he be a single parent and earn a comfortable living at the same time? These are just a few of the many questions that needed answers.

Through in-session exercises and background discovery assignments, William came to understand his motivations with a clarity that enabled him not only to make decisions, but to plan future goals. In a proprietary JMA curriculum called Life RoadMap, William detailed his immediate and long-term aspirations. His objective was ambitious: to provide a peaceful transition for his children while caring for his own needs and starting a new life. William's Life RoadMap served as a guide and benchmark for the journey.

From her facilitation of William's self-discovery, Jody Michael had an understanding of the history and caveats of the situation, but was removed far enough so that she could offer objective insight. She provided support for the day-to-day choices that barraged her client during the legal separation. Ms. Michael carefully directed William toward the goal he had identified in his RoadMap.

Coaching is different from the psychotherapist-client agreement in that it is not ruled by process. Whereas a psychotherapy client benefits from a self-discovery that may meander, the coaching client employs the coach to motivate him to achieve the result he has identified.

Outcome:
William accomplished a successful divorce. More important, he launched a new life in which he was able to care for his children as well as himself. William continued to call on JMA for other emerging issues such as stress management, single parenthood, a new career as an entrepreneur, and a renewed interest in dating and social life.
