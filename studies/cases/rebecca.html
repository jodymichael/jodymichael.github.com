Case Studies :: Career Coaching

Rebecca: I feel like something's missing.

Rebecca gives us a great case study because her story is archetypal of many mid-career professionals we help. The stereotypical midlife crisis recurs in our cultural heritage for good reason: it is an unfortunate and frequent dysfunction of modern life. Life, however, does not have to work this way.

Situation:
When Rebecca came to Jody Michael & Associates, she had been a prolific management consultant in a Fortune 20 firm for eight years. At the time she contacted JMA, she was approximately one year from becoming a partner. However, she had realized that the business world did not contain her calling. Though her outward success was the envy of many, she was not proud of her employment. Rebecca hired Jody Michael & Associates because she was dissatisfied with her career.

Resolution:
During the assessment process, Ms. Michael came to understand that Rebecca was out-of-sorts in more areas of her life than just her work life. JMA assigned Rebecca exercises that allowed her to probe her dysfunctional beliefs, to uncover her self-imposed rules, and to learn about how she had come to be the high-functioning, low-satisfaction working adult that she was. Rebecca diligently worked in-session and on assignments she could complete at her leisure. JMA provided guidance and actionable steps that helped Rebecca work with the personal traits and habits that had been getting in her way.

She saw results quickly. She writes, "Working with Jody, I have been able to find and define my authentic self and begin living my life for me, not for my family or based on what others expected of me." In three to four months, Rebecca turned her life around. She discovered the people and livelihood that fulfilled and refueled her. Among other major life changes, Rebecca left the F20 firm so that she could educate and role-model high school youth in inner-city schools.

Rebecca describes her success: "I cannot remember another time in my life where I have been this happy and at one with myself... I am starting to feel things that I haven't ever allowed myself to feel."
