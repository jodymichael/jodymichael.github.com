Case Studies :: Executive Coaching

John: Bull in a Chia Shop

Situation:
When he came to see Jody Michael & Associates, John was a rising star at a Fortune 100 company. However, after making the first appointment, he asked us why he was seeing us. According to John, everything in his life was great, unless you asked the people that had to work around him. The breaking point occurred when, for the third year in a row, John was denied a promotion to partner due to poor 360° reviews.

Resolution:
John did not want to see Jody Michael & Associates. However, the possibility of not making partner a fourth time was an event he loathed even more. Early in the assessment process, we realized that John was not a good listener. Among other things, he was self-absorbed, a bully, arrogant, anxious, had a temper, and was confrontational. His reviews noted that he was considered a sub-par leader and that he had anger-management issues.

JMA cannot change a client that does not want to change himself. John did not think his behavior needed modification. However, he realized he was hitting a glass ceiling he didn’t like. We explained to him that he had a choice: (1) He could find a firm that promoted those with his personality traits, or (2) He needed to learn how to work, lead, and communicate as his peers and coworkers requested. He could improve his chances of making partner either way. John decided to try the latter approach.

Jody Michael engineered exercises and assignments that attacked John’s belief system. He had grown up the youngest of five boys. Thus, he learned early that life was not fair, that he had to fight for what he wanted, and that bullying led to the spoils rather than to punishment. He had brought this approach into the business world, where the rules were different. In session, Ms. Michael worked with John so that he got practice in the situations that usually brought on his unruly behavior. He learned management techniques, how to read people, how to work a room, and how to discover what others thought of him.

Outcome:
John realized that he needed to bring his focus outside of himself and onto the people around him. However, while John successfully achieved a change in how he addressed situations, he realized the goal that brought him to JMA in the first place. The next year, his 360° reviews improved markedly and his firm promoted him to partner. One of his co-workers went so far as to say that she had witnessed a "remarkable transformation."
