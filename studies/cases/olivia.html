Olivia: New Managerial Skills

Situation:
As frequently happens, this next case begins with a referral. A couple months into the promotion of an employee (Olivia) to management, Ascend Company realized that Olivia had difficulty developing suitable relationships with her team. Up to this point, Olivia was a stellar employee. She had rotated successfully through many departments.

Olivia was a newly promoted manager at a mid-sized provider of fasteners we will call The Ascend Company . After four years, she had rotated successfully through several departments. She began as a cost accountant, spent some time observing the sales and marketing function, and was now working in the warehouse as an interim manager of shipping and receiving while the incumbent manager was on maternity leave. This temporary assignment offered Olivia her first experience leading a team in the business world-and provided The Ascend Company a benign test of Olivia's management skills.

In the first month of the new assignment, Ascend management realized that Olivia had difficulty developing suitable relationships with her team. Even though the department was small, and the situation of her promotion unique, Ascend was concerned to learn of Olivia's communication deficiencies in a leadership setting. She had been an outstanding employee, but she would be limited to non-management roles unless something was done. At a meeting where the performance of the shipping function, was being discussed, one of the regional sales managers suggested the company call Jody Michael & Associates.

Resolution:
In their first meeting, Jody Michael assured Olivia that though The Ascend Company was paying the bill, anything that they discussed would remain confidential. Furthermore, referring to the work as 'management development,' Olivia felt better about the process and its goal. It was not a critique or judgment of her performance, but an investment in her future at the company. She committed to work with JMA, at normal cost to Ascend, for three months of one session per week.

Ms. Michael's assessment of Olivia, as well as her experience of her client, was that she had difficulty extending herself to others. In session, coach and client completed interactive exercises that built Olivia's ability to receive negative feedback, to constructively approach co-workers with whom she was disappointed or angry, and to plan and lead meetings. As a result of the confidentiality clause in their contract, Olivia was able to discuss issues that occurred principally in her life outside of work, but that had a residual affect on the job.

Outcome:
Through a combination of diligent introspection, immersion training in communication and leadership skills, and her ability to immediately practice her new tools on the job, Olivia progressed quickly. JMA's Life RoadMap curriculum demonstrated the successful interaction of life and work. It showed her how to set goals and achieve them while maintaining the recovery time she needed for herself. Her new awareness of how family issues carried over into her performance on the job encouraged her to seek her own outside assistance for those challenges.

Almost as an after-affect, but a requirement that brought this whole process about, Olivia altered her internal dialogue to enhance her self-esteem. As a result, she exhibited the confidence team members expect of a leader, and removed the closed, protective shroud that had made collaboration difficult.

Olivia's improvement through the final weeks of her interim assignment encouraged Ascend's management to give her another position of responsibility; Manufacturing Manager. As a result of her experience with Jody Michael & Associates, her hard work, and her willingness to work through her weaknesses, Olivia's career in management has continued.
